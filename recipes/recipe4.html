Biriyani

A fragrant and flavorful rice dish with spices, meat, and vegetables.


Ingredients:

  • 500g basmati rice
  • 300g chicken or mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons biriyani masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh mint and cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water or chicken/mutton stock
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • Whole spices (cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves) for tempering

Instructions:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing later.
  4. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies to the pot. Sauté for another minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biriyani masala. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add chicken or mutton pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Stir in yogurt, mint, and cilantro leaves. Cook for a few minutes until the meat is well coated with spices.
  8. Add the soaked rice to the pot and gently mix everything together.
  9. Pour in water or stock, season with salt, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid.
  10. Once done, fluff the biriyani gently with a fork. Garnish with fried onions, mint leaves, and cilantro.
  11. Serve hot with raita or salad on the side.

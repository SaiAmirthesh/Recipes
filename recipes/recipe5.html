Parotta

A flaky, layered Indian flatbread, perfect with curries or as a snack.




Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • Optional: Chopped herbs (like cilantro or mint) for flavor


Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour and salt. Add the oil or ghee and mix well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft, pliable dough. Cover and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into equal portions (about the size of a golf ball).
  4. Roll each portion into a thin circle on a floured surface.
  5. Apply a little oil or ghee on the rolled dough, then fold it in layers to form a spiral shape.
  6. Flatten the spiral gently with your palm, then roll it out again into a thin circle.
  7. Heat a skillet over medium heat. Cook each parotta for about 2-3 minutes on each side, adding oil or ghee as needed until golden brown and flaky.
  8. Serve hot with your favorite curry or as a snack.
